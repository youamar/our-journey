Advisor’s Note — IMPORTANT

August in Rome is brutal — 35–38°C and humid. Don’t do 5 monuments straight on foot. Mornings (before 11am) and evenings (after 5pm); rest at the hostel in the middle. Heatstroke ruins trips.

Eat in Trastevere, never near the monuments. Tourist-strip food is overpriced and bad — a 15-min walk doubles the quality and halves the price.

Decide on the morning of, based on weather. The Faulhorn → Schynige Platte hike is 16km, 6+ hours, with serious climbing. In rain or low cloud it’s dangerous and pointless (no view). Check meteoswiss.ch the night before.

Easy version (First → Bachalpsee → back, 2 hours) is equally beautiful. Don’t feel like you have to be a hero.

Before We Fly

The Prep Checklist.

Everything to lock in before August so the trip runs smoothly — most savings come from booking early.

90 days
Book Italian high-speed trains
Roma → Firenze, Firenze → Milano. Frecciarossa/Italo €19 advance vs €70+ same-day. Book on trenitalia.com or italotreno.it.
60 days
Book Colosseum + Vatican tickets
August dates sell out. Combo Colosseum/Forum/Palatine €18 on coopculture.it. Vatican opens slots 60 days ahead.
60 days
Reserve all hostels (non-refundable)
10 nights total. Non-refundable rates are ~10% cheaper. Mountain Hostel Gimmelwald and Balmers fill up fastest.
30 days
Buy Swiss Half Fare Card
CHF 120 online at sbb.ch. Cuts every Swiss train / cable car / lift by 50% for Days 5–11. Pays itself off in 2 days.
14 days
Set up eSIM (Airalo)
Europe-wide 10GB plan ~RM 80. Or grab a local SIM at FCO arrivals — TIM/Vodafone counter, ~€20.
14 days
Travel insurance + EU adaptor
Type C/F plug for both Italy and Switzerland. Insurance: AXA / Allianz, ~RM 200 for 11 days.
7 days
Notify bank + load Wise / Revolut
Wise multi-currency card has the best EUR/CHF rates. Skip Malaysian bank cards — 3% FX fee each swipe.
3 days
Pack light · layers · sneakers
Carry-on only. Alps drop to 10°C even in August. Decent sneakers handle every hike — no boots needed. Refillable water bottle is mandatory.
